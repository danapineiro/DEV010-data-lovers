Tarot is a system of divination and reflection that utilizes a set of cards known as "tarot cards." Each card features images and symbols representing aspects of human experience and the universe at large. Tarot is used to gain insights into past, present, or future situations, as well as to explore emotional, spiritual, and psychological themes. A Tarot deck typically consists of 78 cards, divided into two groups: the Major Arcana and the Minor Arcana. The Major Arcana comprises 22 numbered cards from 0 to 21, each with its own name and symbolism, such as "The Magician," "The Empress," "The Fool," among others. These cards often represent profound and significant life aspects.
